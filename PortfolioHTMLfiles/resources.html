Photos for Your Project

For a one-of-kind photo, hire a local photographer! When that is not possible, these are my favorite sources for buying the right to use a photo (a license): bigstockphoto.com, stockfresh.com, fotolia.com, 123rf.com, istockphoto.com, and shutterstock.com. It's important to understand that other's can buy a license too and you may see the same photo used elsewhere.

With stock photos, you get what you pay for. Rather than spending hours looking through marginal free photos, find what is needed quickly through reputable stock image sources, such as these. I believe in paying fellow artists for their work. It's how we make a living!

Before purchasing a stock image:

• Read and understand copyright and licensing requirements.

• Consider the largest image size needed. You can downsize a poster image for use on a business card, but not the reverse.

Need help understanding image resolution and sizes, here's a helpful article.

WARNING: Just because a photo (or anything else for that matter) is on the Internet, does NOT make it free for the taking. Don't risk being sued. Use only properly licensed images.
